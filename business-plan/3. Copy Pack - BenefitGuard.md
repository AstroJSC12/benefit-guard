## LEAD MAGNET — FULL OUTLINE

### The Denied Claim Action Plan: What to Do in the Next 24 Hours
*4-page rapid-response guide delivered via email after quiz completion. Replaces the original 14-page Denied Claim Playbook. Templates removed (competitors offer AI-generated personalized letters for free). Value is in the PROCESS, CONFIDENCE, and PHONE SCRIPTS.*

**Page 1 — Read Your Denial Letter (What to Look For)**
- What a denial letter is required to tell you by law
- The 4 most common denial reasons and what they really mean (one sentence each):
  1. "Not medically necessary" — Your insurer disagrees with your doctor. You can challenge this.
  2. "Out of network" — Check if the No Surprises Act protects you. It often does.
  3. "Not a covered benefit" — Request the specific policy language. Sometimes they're wrong.
  4. "Prior authorization not obtained" — This may be your provider's error, not yours.
- Visual callout: where to find the deadline, reason code, and appeal instructions on the letter
- Write down 3 things: what was denied, why, and your deadline to respond

**Page 2 — Make This Phone Call (Exact Script)**
- Who to call: the number on your denial letter or the back of your insurance card
- What to say: "I'm calling about a denied claim. My reference number is [X]. Can you tell me the specific clinical criteria you used to deny this claim?"
- What to ask for: the clinical criteria, the reviewer's name, and the specific policy language cited
- What to write down: everything. Word for word. Get a reference number.
- Why this works: it signals you're not going away, and it gives you the exact evidence you need for your appeal

**Page 3 — Your Rights in 60 Seconds**
- **ACA:** You have the legal right to an internal appeal and an independent external review. This is federal law. Your insurer cannot opt out.
- **No Surprises Act:** If you received emergency care or were treated at an in-network facility by an out-of-network provider, you're protected from surprise bills.
- **Mental Health Parity Act:** Mental health and substance use benefits must be covered on par with medical/surgical benefits. If your behavioral health claim was denied, this law may protect you.
- One sentence: "You have more legal protection than your insurance company wants you to know."

**Page 4 — Next Steps**
- Step 1: File your internal appeal (your insurer must tell you how in the denial letter)
- Step 2: If denied again, request an external review (independent third party — overturns 40-60% of the time)
- Step 3: File a complaint with your state's Department of Insurance (free, and they investigate)
- Step 4: For personalized help with your specific plan and situation → try BenefitGuard
- Closing line: "You shouldn't need a law degree to use your health insurance. BenefitGuard is the AI copilot that makes sure you don't."
