  1. "Not medically necessary"
  2. "Out of network"
  3. "Not a covered benefit"
  4. "Prior authorization not obtained"
- How to find the specific denial code and reason (with visual callouts showing where to look on a real denial letter)

**Pages 5-8 — The 5-Step Appeal Process**

- **Step 1: Don't panic. Read the letter carefully.**
  - What to look for: deadline (usually 180 days for internal appeal), reason code, appeal instructions
  - Write down three things: what was denied, why, and the deadline to respond

- **Step 2: Call your insurance company (use this script).**
  - Exact phone script: what to say, what to ask for, what to write down
  - Ask for: the clinical criteria used, the reviewer's name, and the specific policy language they're citing
  - Pro tip: always get a reference number

- **Step 3: Gather your evidence.**
  - What counts as evidence: doctor's letter of medical necessity, test results, treatment records, clinical guidelines
  - How to ask your doctor for a letter of support (sample request email included)

- **Step 4: Write and send your appeal letter.**
  - The anatomy of a winning appeal letter (broken down section by section)
  - Reference to the 3 templates in the appendix
  - Where to send it, how to send it (certified mail vs. fax vs. portal), and why to keep copies of everything

- **Step 5: Know your next moves if they say no again.**
  - External review: what it is, how to request it, and why it works (insurers overturn denials 40-60% of the time at external review)
  - State insurance department complaints: when and how to file
  - The nuclear option: when to consider a lawyer or patient advocate

**Pages 9-11 — Appeal Letter Templates**

- **Template A: "Not Medically Necessary" Appeal**
  - Fill-in-the-blank format with guidance notes
  - Cites ACA Section 2719 internal claims and appeals requirements
  - Includes space for doctor's supporting statement

- **Template B: "Out of Network" Appeal**
  - References No Surprises Act protections
  - Applicable when emergency services or in-network facility with out-of-network provider
  - Includes balance billing dispute language

- **Template C: "Not a Covered Benefit" Appeal**
  - References plan's Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC)
  - Includes language for requesting the specific policy exclusion in writing
  - Mental Health Parity Act language for behavioral health denials

**Page 12 — Know Your Rights (Cheat Sheet)**
- ACA: Right to internal appeal + external review for all marketplace and employer plans
- No Surprises Act: Emergency services, out-of-network at in-network facilities, air ambulance
- Mental Health Parity Act: Mental health and substance use benefits must be on par with medical/surgical
- ERISA: Federal protections for employer-sponsored plans
- Your state may give you even more protections (link to state-by-state resource)

**Page 13 — State Insurance Regulator Contacts**
- Table: all 50 states + DC with department name, phone number, and complaint filing URL
